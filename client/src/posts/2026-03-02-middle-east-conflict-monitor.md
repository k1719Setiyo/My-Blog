It's getting harder to get a factual, real-time picture of what's happening across the Middle East. News is moving faster than ever. With the current wave of dynamic airspace updates, it’s difficult to tell which corridors are actually open and which reports are verified. Official statements often get buried under social media noise, and international media doesn't always capture the granular details as quickly as local sources do.

So I spent the weekend building the **[ME Conflict Monitor](https://36889091.streaklinks.com/CysvXbDcnPos69pCwgrTIer5/https%3A%2F%2Fwww.meconfmonitor.com)**: a live news and data aggregator to organize and prioritize raw reporting from the region. I originally built it for myself to stay on top of everything and extract signal from noise, but I figured it could also be a helpful resource for others, so I published it as a public good that anyone can use.

The site pulls from hundreds of sources and lets you easily look at reporting and status across each country through four categories:

* **Airspace Status & NOTAMs:** A live tracker of regional flight restrictions and official Notices to Air Missions (NOTAMs). This provides a technical, real-time look at airspace closures and security alerts issued by civil aviation authorities including their expiration times.
* **Flight Tracker:** The tracker covers 13 tracked airports. Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Doha, Bahrain, Riyadh, Jeddah, Medina, Dammam, Muscat, Tel Aviv, Beirut, Amman, and Aqaba. 
* **Domestic News:** Local reporting that often captures details and breaking developments faster than global outlets. Some domestic sources may have government affiliations, but they're essential for understanding the local narrative.
* **Government Feeds:** Official statements and data directly from the source. Understanding each government's official position is essential to seeing the full picture.
* **International News:** Major global media for a broader perspective.

I built this as a "news only" tool; you see the reports and data exactly as they're released, without external interpretation or commentary. The site is transparent and ever-evolving, and you can view the full source list and data feeds directly on the page.
